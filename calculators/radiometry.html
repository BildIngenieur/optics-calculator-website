Radiometry — Camera Equation

On-axis irradiance at the image plane from an extended Lambertian source.

Camera Equation (on-axis, Lambertian source)
$$E = \frac{\pi\,L\,\tau}{4\,F_w^2} \quad \left[\text{W/m}^2\right]$$ where $L$ = source radiance [W/(m²·sr)], $\tau$ = lens transmission, $F_w$ = working F-number
$F_w = (1-m)\,F\!\#$   ($m \le 0$ for real image from real object; $m = 0$ for object at infinity)
Photometric equivalent: replace $L$ with luminance $L_v$ [cd/m²] to obtain illuminance $E_v$ [lux]. Convert: $E\,[\text{W/m}^2] = E_v\,[\text{lux}]\,/\,K_m\,[\text{lm/W}]$.
Total flux in image plane: $\;\Phi = E \times A_\text{image}$  [W]
