Angular Mapping

Plate scale, pixels per degree, and angular field of view for a camera–lens system.

Formulas
$$\alpha = \frac{p}{f} \;\text{rad/px}$$ where $p$ = pixel pitch and $f$ = effective focal length (same units). Valid to better than 0.01% for $p \ll f$.
$\alpha_{\text{arcsec}} = \dfrac{p}{f} \times \dfrac{648\,000}{\pi}$   $\text{px/deg} = \dfrac{f}{p} \times \dfrac{\pi}{180}$
$\alpha_\text{mm} = \dfrac{1}{f}$ rad/mm   (image-plane linear plate scale; independent of pixel pitch)
$$\text{FOV} = 2\arctan\!\!\left(\frac{N \cdot p}{2f}\right)$$ where $N$ = number of pixels along that dimension (exact formula)
